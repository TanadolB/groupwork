Smart Grid Architecture

Job Description: The Smart Grid Architect focuses on the intersection of energy and digital infrastructure.
You will design the technical frameworks for modernising power distribution, integrating distributed energy resources (DERs)
like solar and wind, and enabling two-way communication between the grid and smart meters for real-time monitoring and demand response.

  • Reference: SG612
  • Salary: $155,000–$195,000 AUD
  • Reporting Line: Director of Energy Systems

Key Responsibilities

  1. Designing end-to-end technical architectures for urban IoT deployments.
  2. Ensuring interoperability between legacy city infrastructure and new digital platforms.
  3. Conducting impact assessments to measure the ROI of smart technology investments.
  4. Cleaning and managing "Big Data" sets to ensure accuracy in automated city services.

Required Skills

  1. Essential: Degree in Electrical Engineering or Power Systems; 5+ years in power systems design; expertise in SCADA systems.
  2. Preferable: Experience with IEEE 2030.5 standards; BESS (Battery Energy Storage) experience.

Project Manager

Job Description: You will lead the end-to-end delivery of digital platforms for councils and industry partners. This role involves managing the deployment of smart transport and energy monitoring systems, ensuring projects are completed on time, within budget, and meet the specific urban needs of Australian communities.

  • Reference: PM305
  • Salary: $130,000–$165,000 AUD.
  • Reporting Line: Head of Operations

Key Responsibilities

  1. Manage the project lifecycle for urban services management platforms
  2. Act as the primary point of contact for council stakeholders and technology vendors.
  3. Track project KPIs, including budget utilization and infrastructure deployment milestones.
  4. Identify and mitigate risks related to physical hardware installation and software integration.

Required Skills

  1. Essential: Degree in Project Management, Business, or IT; 3+ years experience in infrastructure or technology projects; PMBOK or PRINCE2 certification.
  2. Preferable: Experience working with Australian local government procurement; background in IoT or smart city technologies; strong contract negotiation skills.
